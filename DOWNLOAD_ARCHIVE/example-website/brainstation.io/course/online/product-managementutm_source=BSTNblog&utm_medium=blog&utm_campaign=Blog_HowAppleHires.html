    UNIT 1

    Product Exploration & Strategy

    You'll start by learning the foundations of product management, including the product spectrum, customer-centricity, Lean product process, and the role of a Product Manager. As you complete this Product Management certification, you'll learn how to perform opportunity and customer discovery, and how to translate your research insights into a product vision and value proposition hypothesis that can be applied to software product management and more.

    • Google Forms
    • SurveyMonkey
    KEY SKILLS:
    Problem Exploration
    Customer & Market Research
    Product Strategy
    Team Leadership

    Understand Your Customers

    Learn to perform effective customer research as a Product Manager and define your target customers as personas to inform your customer-centric product management strategy.

    Discover the Lean Product Process

    Learn the core principles of Lean, centered around eliminating waste, remaining flexible, ensuring Agile delivery, and more.

    Identify Product Management Opportunities

    Learn to identify the problem space versus the solution space to help structure your thinking, capitalize on opportunities, and inform your product management iterations.

    Develop Product Management Hypotheses

    Incorporate everything you learn into a product management vision and value proposition hypothesis that will form the foundation of your product management strategy.

    UNIT 2

    Agile Product Development

    Go deeper into Agile development and Lean methodologies and discover how they work in tandem to enable product management teams to move quickly, and rapidly iterate toward product-market fit and an effective product. Learn how Product Managers work with development teams through the details of Agile development practices, including the core Agile principles, Agile variations, and how to prepare user stories and complete story mapping.

    KEY SKILLS:
    Agile Methodology
    Product Planning
    MVPs
    Agile Project Management

    Drive the Development of Efficient MVPs

    Learn how Product Managers apply the Build-Measure-Learn framework of Lean to develop efficient Minimum Viable Products (MVPs) that test your product hypothesis and allow you to iterate toward an effective product – a common practice in digital product management.

    Learn to Lead Development with Agile Methodologies

    Learn why Product Managers leverage Agile methodologies and explore how to incorporate them into your product development in order to deliver better solutions faster.

    Discover the Pivot: What It Is and When to Use It

    The concept of a pivot, informed by Lean methodologies, has been essential in the development of many product innovations. As a Product Manager, learn how to apply pivots to your product management process through exploring examples of successful pivots that could apply throughout the product life cycle.

    UNIT 3

    Building and Refining Products

    The best products are developed through iterative product management processes that allow businesses to experiment and learn while constantly improving the product. Learn how Product Managers apply wireframing, testing, prioritization, and roadmapping as part of their product strategy to build effective products within their product management program.

    KEY SKILLS:
    Product Prototyping
    Wireframing
    User Testing
    Iterative Product Development

    Discover the Essential Role Wireframing Plays in Product Management

    Learn how a Product Manager uses wireframing as part of rapid prototyping to design more effective, customer-centric products.

    Learn the Step-by-Step Testing Plan to Inform Your Product Efforts

    Iterative development requires effective testing. Learn the steps to set up and execute valuable tests, including usability tests, that drive your product management iterations.

    Define Priorities and Your Product Management Roadmap

    By applying time-tested prioritization strategies, learn how a Product Manager focuses on the most important aspects of any product. Learn to develop a product management roadmap to drive successful product execution.

    UNIT 4

    Launch Strategy

    When a product has completed development, it's time for Product Managers to prepare for launch. Explore product marketing strategies used by Product Managers to help drive a successful launch, as well as the business analytics needed to monitor the launch and ongoing growth as part of a complete product management program.

    KEY SKILLS:
    Product Positioning
    Go-To-Market Strategy
    Pricing Strategy
    Risk Management
    Product Analytics

    Learn Successful Product Marketing

    Understand what product marketing is and how to develop your positioning, messaging, and brand strategy to effectively market your product.

    Apply Effective Revenue Models and Pricing Strategies

    Explore common revenue models and the pricing strategies that work with them to drive value for both your business and your customers.

    Implement Analytics for Data-Driven Decision Making

    Discover the key stages of Lean analytics to help track both the success of your launch and ongoing growth and engagement.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    • What is the difference between Product Managers and a Product Owner?

      A Product Manager is responsible for discovering and understanding what the user is looking for, and then using those insights to guide product development. Product Managers often work in software, as software Product Managers, but can be seen in all kinds of industries and businesses.

      This is closely related to a Product Owner, who is in charge of maximizing the value of the product by ensuring that the team is always optimizing to meet user needs. This includes creating and managing a backlog to both track feature requests and implement those features.

      Both of these differ from Product Marketing, in which a Product Marketer is responsible for positioning the product in a way that best suits the market. Further, all of these roles work closely with product design, which envisions and optimizes the user experience of the product.

    • How advanced is the Product Management certification course?

      In this Product Management certification course, you'll learn to prioritize problems and find user-oriented solutions, identify market opportunities and generally learn and get a strong overview of how to build successful products with Agile teams. Through case studies, lessons, and a final project, this course offers a great hands-on training for aspiring product managers.

      While in-depth, the Product Management course can be considered beginner-friendly and is open to anyone looking to learn more about product management or take steps toward a product management career.

    • Are there any prerequisites for this product management course?

      BrainStation's Product Management course was created for those who are new to product management, digital products, or product marketing. The course can be considered beginner-friendly and has no prerequisites.

    • Will I earn a Product Management certificate from this course?

      Yes, when you complete the course, you will earn a Product Management certification. Product Management certifications can boost your LinkedIn profile and resume, helping you stand out in the job market when applying for Product Manager positions. BrainStation's Product Management certification is formatted for easy sharing on LinkedIn.

    • What kind of backgrounds do people have that take this course?

      By enrolling in the Product Management course, you can expect to learn from Instructors and certified Product Management professionals with experience in the field, and to collaborate with like-minded, ambitious professionals keen to become a Product Manager or build Product Manager skills.

      From an educational point of view, 80 percent of learners at BrainStation have a post-secondary education, with 20 percent having earned a Master's degree or PhD.

      Professional backgrounds vary as the demand for product management skills increases. In fact, according to the BrainStation Digital Skills Survey, 86 percent of respondents working in product started their careers in a different field. This is unsurprising, as there is not one single path to becoming a Product Manager.

    • What Product Management methods do we learn?

      Throughout the Product Management course, you will learn to navigate systems essential to the role of a Product Manager. You will also gain hands-on experience with Agile and Scrum processes: often used by Product Managers to manage the development and launch of a product and its features.
